Tracing back to raw materials

  • Glass: Smooth and durable surface for the display
  • Liquid crystals: Contort with electric current
  • LED Backlight: Semiconductors (GaAs, GaP)
  • Plastic: External frame and casing
  • Metals: Structural component, electronics
  • Electronics: ICs, transistors, capacitors, resistors
  • Adhesives/Sealants: Bond layers of LCD screen
  • Inorganic: ITO for transparent conductive coatings

Glass

  • Brittle, transparent, non-crystalline solid
  • Sand melted at about 1600Â°C
  • SiO2 (Quartz), limestone, soda-lime etc are used
  • Glass formed by floating molten glass molten tin bed
  • Annealed, laminated and cut into sheets

Liquid Crystals (LC)

  • Organic compounds with properties of typical liquids and solid crystals
  • Research fuelled by Richard Williams, 1962, to replace CRTs
  • Long, rod-shaped molecules
  • Electric field changes orientation of molecules
  • Polarized light passes through liquid crystal

Plastic

  • Mainly used for making external casing
  • The versatile thermoplastic Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) is commonly used
  • Durable, impact resistant, mouldable, lightweight
  • Surface finish, resistant to chemical cleaning agents
  • Obtained from crude oil or natural gas. Refined oil is cracked to break large hydrocarbons and then polymerized to form plastics

Aluminium (Metal)

  • Used for structural components and electronics
  • Aluminium is commonly used, obtained from Bauxite
  • Bauxite ore is crushed and refined to form Alumina (aluminium oxide) by Bayer's process
  • Alumina is converted to useful aluminium using Hall-Heroult process
  • Lightweight, corrosion resistant, good conductor of heat and electricity

Reduce

  • New research in semiconductor and materials to make energy-efficient components
  • Having integrated components like speakers or power supplies reduces resources used
  • Streamlined design, leaving out unnessary gimmicks

Reuse

  • Repurposing old displays in innovative ways
  • Modular design, eg Framework Laptops, to enhance repairablilty, upgradability
  • Salvaging components

Recycle

  • Exchange programs
  • Components like casing and circuit boards can be recycled separately
  • Donation and sale instead of hoarding
  • Manufacture buy-back
